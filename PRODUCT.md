# Product

## Register

product

## Users

This is a personal technical knowledge base for an AI infrastructure learner/operator who needs to quickly understand projects, architecture boundaries, and selection tradeoffs across LLM serving, AI agents, cloud native systems, Kubernetes GPU/device stacks, and code intelligence tooling.

## Product Purpose

llm-wiki turns raw source material and codebase ingest notes into linked, navigable wiki pages. Success means a reader can move from a broad topic to the right concept, project map, source analysis, or entity page without scanning a long undifferentiated document.

## Brand Personality

Dense, pragmatic, and research-oriented. The interface should feel like a serious technical workspace: calm enough for repeated use, structured enough for comparison, and direct enough to support fast project understanding.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-site hero layouts, decorative card grids, vague AI-tool aesthetics, oversized typography, and pages that hide useful technical links behind promotional copy. The wiki should not feel like a SaaS landing page or a visual demo at the expense of navigation.

## Design Principles

- Prioritize wayfinding over decoration.
- Make comparison and selection paths explicit.
- Keep high information density, but preserve scanning structure.
- Treat source pages, entity pages, concept pages, and analysis maps as distinct reading jobs.
- Preserve Obsidian-style wikilink semantics while improving the generated HTML experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target readable contrast in both dark and light themes, keyboard-reachable navigation, semantic headings, stable responsive layouts, and reduced reliance on motion. The site should remain usable as a static HTML knowledge base.
